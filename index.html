AST-101 — Introduction to Apostolic Theology

Bronze — Associate of Theology (A.Th.) · 3 Credit Hours · Course Code: AST-101

A. Course Description

This course introduces Apostolic (Oneness Pentecostal) theology as rooted in Scripture and expressed in historic Apostolic movements. Emphasis is placed on Oneness of God, Acts 2:38 salvation, holiness of life, and authority of the Apostolic Scriptures.

B. Course Objectives

  1. Define “Apostolic theology” and “Apostolic doctrine” biblically.
  2. Explain the meaning of “the apostles’ doctrine” (Acts 2:42).
  3. Articulate the Oneness of God with key Scriptures.
  4. Explain Apostolic plan of salvation (repentance, Jesus’ Name baptism, Spirit baptism).
  5. Outline basic doctrine of holiness in lifestyle and conduct.
  6. Differentiate Apostolic theology from Trinitarianism and generic evangelical beliefs.
  7. Demonstrate understanding of early church identity and continuity with modern Apostolic believers.

C. Detailed Course Outline

Unit 1 – What Does “Apostolic” Mean?

  • Biblical Use of “Apostle”: Definition of apostolos (one sent). The Twelve Apostles and their unique role.
  • The Apostles’ Doctrine – Acts 2:42: What early believers continued in; doctrine as teaching.
  • “Apostolic” as Theological Identity: Being Apostolic vs. merely Pentecostal; difference from charismatic belief.

Unit 2 – Authority of Scripture and the Apostolic Witness

  • Inspiration and Inerrancy of Scripture (2 Tim. 3:16–17).
  • Apostolic Witness of Christ — eyewitnesses and 1 Cor 15.
  • Canon and Apostolic Writings — why New Testament books are Apostolic; dangers of extra-biblical distortions.

Unit 3 – The Oneness of God (Apostolic Christology)

  • Biblical Monotheism (Deut 6:4; Isaiah).
  • God manifest in the flesh (1 Tim 3:16; John 1:1,14; Colossians 2:9).
  • Refutation of Trinity as three persons; Sonship as incarnation.
  • Jesus as the Name of God in the New Covenant — harmony of Matt 28:19 & Acts 2:38.

Unit 4 – Apostolic Plan of Salvation (Acts 2:38 Pattern)

  • Repentance (metanoia).
  • Water baptism in the name of Jesus (immersion) — Acts examples.
  • Spirit baptism with evidence of tongues (Acts 2,10,19).
  • Obedient response to the Gospel — Romans 6 imagery.

Unit 5 – Apostolic Holiness and Lifestyle

  • Holiness as God’s nature (1 Peter 1:15–16).
  • Inward holiness: heart, attitudes, Fruit of the Spirit.
  • Outward holiness: modesty, sobriety, conduct.
  • Holiness as identity, not mere legalism.

Unit 6 – Apostolic Church, Mission, and Continuity

  • Marks of the Apostolic Church (Acts 2:42): doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, prayers.
  • Historical continuity of Jesus-name witnesses; restoration leading to modern Pentecost.
  • Modern Apostolic movements and missionary vision.
  • Living Apostolic today: calling, ministry prep, guarding the Apostolic deposit (2 Tim. 1:13–14).
